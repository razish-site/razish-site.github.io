Zishaan and Razia would like to invite you to celebrate their marriage.

We hope you can join us to celebrate our marriage. As we both love a party we are having two, quite different, events on subsequent Saturday’s in July. The first is a traditional English wedding (white dress and morning suits) and the second will bring in elements of our cultures (Zambian, Indian and Pakistani). We hope that as you join us, in either of the celebrations, you will be able to get a glimpse of who we are, our cultures, interests (food) and most importantly our love for each other. So bring your dancing shoes and come and celebrate with us.

Our story began in March 2012 when Razia walked into her usual room in the university Economics block in Bristol only to find the geek who loved debating with lecturers there. Zishaan tried his hardest to start conversation even so far as to sing Beyonce songs. Somewhere between Beyonce and arguing about development economics, Zishaan managed to steal Razia’s heart - study partners, dinner dates and lots of laughter, a true friendship blossomed.

Razia went to LSE for her masters and Zishaan went to Birmingham, but they would meet again in London for lunch, which incidentally fell on Valentines Day, and the rest as they say is history.
